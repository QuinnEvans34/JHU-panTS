# M1P1: Project Pitch & Defense

> **Due:** Thursday by 11:59pm · **Points:** 30 · **Submit:** website URL or file upload · **Available until:** Jul 9 at 11:59pm

## Overview

You will pitch your project to the class and defend it under questioning. The goal is not a polished slide deck — it is a clear, confident explanation of what you are building and why it is worth building. Your peers will act as a business review panel.

## Instructions

### Presenter — deliver a 5-minute pitch covering:

1. The problem and the business user.
2. The dataset and why it is suitable.
3. The ML approach and what the output will look like.
4. The front-end and how a business user will consume it.
5. One thing you are personally motivated by or excited to learn.

Be prepared for **5 minutes of Q&A** from the class and instructor. You will be evaluated on how clearly and confidently you respond — not on whether your answers are perfect.

### Audience (every student not currently presenting):

During each pitch, take structured notes and be an active participant:

- Ask at least **2 meaningful questions** across all presentations.
- Your questions should probe business relevance, data validity, technical feasibility, or scope realism.

After class, submit an **Audience Notes Document** covering every presentation you observed. For each project, include:

- A 2–3 sentence summary of the project.
- The question(s) you asked.
- A brief assessment: what is strong about this proposal, and what is unclear or risky?

## Learning Outcomes

- Communicate and defend technical work to a non-technical audience.
- Manage an independent project from proposal to delivery.

## Deliverables

- **Presenter:** In-class presentation. Approval is granted or withheld by the instructor at the end of reviewing the actual proposal submitted through M1A1.
- **Audience:** `audience-notes-week1.md`.

## Rubric (30 pts)

| Criterion | Role | Description | Pts |
|-----------|------|-------------|-----|
| Clarity of Problem & Business Value | Presenter | The problem, business user, and value of the ML system are communicated clearly enough that any audience member could explain them back. | 5 |
| Dataset & Approach Justification | Presenter | Dataset source, update frequency, and ML approach are explained and justified. The presenter can speak to why this data supports this approach. | 5 |
| Q&A Confidence & Depth | Presenter | Responses are direct, honest about uncertainty where it exists, and demonstrate genuine understanding of the project. | 5 |
| Delivery & Professionalism | Presenter | Confident, maintains time, speaks to the audience rather than reading, and carries themselves professionally. | 5 |
| Question Quality | Audience | Questions asked during Q&A are specific and substantive — probing business relevance, data validity, feasibility, or scope. Not surface-level or yes/no. | 5 |
| Audience Notes Quality | Audience | Notes submitted for all presentations. Each entry includes a project summary, questions asked, and a specific strength and risk assessment reflecting genuine critical thinking. | 5 |
