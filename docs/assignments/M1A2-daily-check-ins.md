# M1A2: Daily Check-Ins

> **Due:** Saturday by 11:59pm · **Points:** 10 · **Submit:** website URL or file upload · **Available until:** Jul 11 at 11:59pm

## Overview

Consistent, honest daily reflection is a professional habit — not a busywork requirement. Your `standup-log.md` is a running record of how you worked, what slowed you down, and how you responded to challenges. It lives in your repository and will be reviewed during 1-on-1 check-ins and referenced during your final presentation. Treat it as a document you would be comfortable showing an employer.

## Instructions

Update `standup-log.md` at the end of every weekday (Monday through Friday). Each entry should be brief and honest — three to five sentences total is sufficient. Do not write what you wish you had done. Write what actually happened.

### Standard Daily Entry Format

```markdown
## [Week X — Day] — [Date]
**Worked on:** What did you actually work on today?
**Up next:** What are you working on in the next session?
**Blockers:** Are you facing any challenges or blockers? If none, write "None."
```

### 1-on-1 Retrospective Entry

In Weeks 1, 3, and 5, one of your weekly entries will replace the standard format with a 1-on-1 retrospective. This entry is written after your check-in with the instructor on that day.

```markdown
## [Week X — 1-on-1 Retrospective] — [Date]
**What we discussed:** Briefly summarize the key topics covered in the check-in.
**Feedback received:** What specific feedback or direction did the instructor give?
**Action items:** What will you do differently or prioritize as a result?
**Reflection:** In one or two sentences — was there anything that surprised you or that you want to think more about?
```

## File Location & Format

`standup-log.md` lives in the root `docs/` folder alongside your other living documents. Entries are added chronologically — do not edit or delete previous entries. The log grows across all 5 weeks into a complete record of your project journey.
